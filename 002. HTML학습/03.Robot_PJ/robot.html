Robot

A robot ( Cultural language : robo, English : robot ) is a mechanical device programmed to automatically perform various tasks .

A robot ( Cultural language : robo, English : robot ) is a mechanical device programmed to automatically perform various tasks .

It is a mechanical device that operates with a program (programmable) and performs a complex series of actions that are difficult, complex, and dangerous for humans to perform on their own.

Automated robots that perform assembly, welding, and handling in manufacturing plants such as automobile production lines are called industrial robots , and robots that have the ability to recognize the environment and make decisions on their own are called ' intelligent robots '. There are also food tech robots (meal service robots) that perform cooking tasks in cafeterias such as schools instead of humans .

Robots that resemble humans are called ' androids '. Another meaning is 'form', and they are also called machines with the ability to think for themselves. Robots that use artificial power work instead of or with humans. Usually, robots are designed to do the work planned by the manufacturer.

The term 'robot' was first used by Czechoslovakian playwright Karel Čapek in his 1920 play "RUR", and has since become widely used. The etymology of the word robot is the Czech word robota , meaning "labor", "slavery", or "hard, monotonous work" .
